Neighborhood Guidelines & Reminders

These are general reminders based on HOA documents to help keep Bruce Meadows clean, safe, and enjoyable for everyone. They are not intended as enforcement notices. The official covenants and by-laws remain the governing documents.

Property Appearance

  • Keep yards neat and well maintained.
  • Avoid leaving items, debris, or materials in open view.

Trash & Garbage

  • Store trash containers neatly when not in use.
  • Avoid leaving garbage out longer than necessary.

Exterior Projects

  • Review HOA documents before major exterior changes.
  • Fences, sheds, additions, and other improvements should follow the recorded guidelines.

Pets

  • Household pets are welcome.
  • Please keep pets controlled and respectful of neighbors.

Pond & Common Areas

  • Use common areas responsibly.
  • Avoid unsafe or unauthorized activity in the pond area.

Community Courtesy

  • Be mindful of neighbors and shared spaces.
  • Help keep the neighborhood clean, safe, and welcoming.

Community Rules & Official Reference

Important: This section is a simplified resident-friendly summary. The official by-laws and covenants should always be used as the controlling documents.

How the HOA Operates

  • The by-laws identify the association as the Bruce Meadows Home Owners Association.
  • The HOA has four elected officer positions: President, Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer.
  • The Secretary maintains meeting minutes and handles appropriate correspondence.
  • The Treasurer receives, records, banks, and disburses association funds.
  • The by-laws describe annual meetings, special meetings, voting, committees, dues, assessments, and officer duties.
View By-Laws

Common Covenant Topics

  • Lots are intended for residential use and single-family homes.
  • Rules address building type, dwelling size, outbuildings, signs, fences, animals, garbage, drainage, wells, septic systems, and flood plain requirements.
  • Park, common, and retention areas are addressed in the covenants.
  • The covenants describe enforcement, assessments, liens, and the association's authority.
  • Residents should review the full covenants before major exterior work or property changes.

Frequently Asked Questions

When are HOA dues due?

The 2026 annual association fee is $100 and is due by May 1, 2026. Dues become $125 if paid after May 31.

How do I pay HOA dues?

Pay by check made out to Bruce Meadows Homeowners Association. Include your Lot # on the check. The 2026 annual notice lists mailing/drop-off instructions through the Treasurer.

How do I submit a question, suggestion, or concern?

Use the resident submission form on this site. Submissions are reviewed by the board before any action is taken or information is shared publicly.

Who handles road repairs or patching?

Road patching requests should be directed to Macomb County Road Commission at 586-463-8671.

Where can I find the by-laws, covenants, and plat drawings?

They are available in the Official HOA Documents section of this site.

Are the neighborhood guidelines enforcement notices?

No. The guidelines are general reminders to help residents understand expectations. The official by-laws and covenants remain the governing documents.

Where are HOA announcements posted?

Short notices may appear on this website. Longer announcements, meeting recaps, and neighborhood updates can be posted on the Bruce Meadows HOA WordPress page.
